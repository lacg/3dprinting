## Product Ideas
<!-- Things you could design, print, and sell -->

**Sourcing model:** designs licensed from [MakerWorld](https://makerworld.com) with commercial/sell permission → printed and sold on Etsy. *(Always confirm each model's license explicitly allows selling physical prints before listing.)*

**Paid/Patreon designs are OK** — if a model is good enough to sell, subscribing to the creator's Patreon for the commercial license is an accepted cost of doing business. Just confirm the license stays valid for items already sold if/when the subscription lapses.

**Design decisions:**
- **MagSafe = puck-pocket** — print a recess that holds the buyer's own stick-on MagSafe puck. Simpler BOM, no embedded magnets, buyer supplies the charger.
- **Vases = faux/dry stems** — designed for artificial or dried flowers, so no watertight printing or test-tube insert needed.

## Materials & Equipment Notes
<!-- Printers, filaments/resins, finishing tools you have or want -->
- **Filament by use case:**
  - Outdoor (feeders): **ASA** (best UV/weather) or **PETG** — avoid plain PLA outdoors, it degrades in sun/heat.
  - Indoor decor & desk: **PLA** (easy, cheap, great detail); **silk/matte** PLA for premium look.
  - Anatomy models: **matte PLA** photographs well and hides layer lines.
- **MagSafe pucks:** buyer supplies their own (puck-pocket design) — no magnets to stock.
- **Vase inserts:** none needed (faux/dry stems decision).
- **Finishing/tools to have:** flush cutters, deburring tool, spare nozzles, IPA + brass brush, decent lighting + simple lightbox for product photos.
- **Printer decision (in progress):** debating **Bambu Lab H2C** vs **H2C + 10W laser module**.
  - *Both configs share identical print/build specs* — the 10W laser is the **only** difference. So this is a clean yes/no on laser capability, not a size/quality trade-off.
  - *Base H2C* covers 100% of the current catalog (all FDM prints) — the feeders, stands, vases, and anatomy models don't need a laser.
  - *10W laser* adds an **adjacent product line** (engraving/cutting wood, acrylic, leather → personalized signs, ornaments, coasters, gift tags) — a differentiator, not a requirement.
  - Trade-offs of adding the laser: higher upfront cost, needs **ventilation + fire safety**, separate learning curve, and laser materials/stock to buy. Only worth it if you actually plan to sell engraved/cut goods.
  - **Cost delta:** financing ~$89/mo (base) vs ~$106/mo (laser) over 24 mo = **~$17/mo, ~$408 total** for the laser.
  - **DECISION → buy base H2C now; add a *second* H2C + 10W laser when the business ramps up.** Rationale: defers the laser spend until revenue justifies it, and the second machine then solves two things at once — laser capability **+** doubled FDM capacity — plus redundancy (no single point of failure). Buying the laser up front only wins if you'd want laser without needing more capacity, which won't be the case at ramp-up.
  - **Ramp-up trigger to define:** e.g. consistent backlog / orders-per-week or monthly revenue that keeps the first printer near capacity → signal to buy printer #2 (laser).
- **Printer specs to confirm:** build volume and any size limits that cap which models you can print (esp. larger feeders / full-size brain/skeleton).

## Pricing Thoughts
<!-- Cost of material + time + markup; subscription vs. one-off -->

### Cost formula (per item)
```
Unit cost   = material + machine/electricity + labor + packaging
Etsy fees   = ~16% effective (listing $0.20 + 6.5% transaction + ~3% payment
              + Etsy Ads / Offsite Ads ≈ 12–15% during launch push)
Retail      = (unit cost ÷ (1 − target margin)) , then add fees on top
```
> **Note:** Using **16% effective Etsy fees** for now — planning to run Etsy Ads early to gain visibility, which raises the blended fee. Dial this back toward ~10% once organic traffic/reviews carry sales and ad spend drops.
Rule-of-thumb inputs (tune to your actuals):
- **Filament:** ~$22/kg → **$0.022/g** (PLA). ASA/PETG outdoor ~$26/kg → **$0.026/g**
- **Failure/waste buffer:** +10% on material
- **Machine + power:** ~$0.10/hr of print time
- **Labor (finishing/QC/packing):** your hourly rate × hands-on minutes (start ~$20/hr → $0.33/min)
- **Packaging:** ~$1.00–2.00/order
- **Target margin:** 60–70% before Etsy fees
