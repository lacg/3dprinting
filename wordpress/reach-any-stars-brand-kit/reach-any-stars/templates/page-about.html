Everything starts as a thought: a shape you picture, a gift you can already see, an idea you can’t quite shake. Reach Any Stars exists to close the distance between imagining it and holding it.

We believe in better than before. In making, not mass-producing. In things that mean something because they were made for you, not for everyone.

So we print to order. You choose the piece and the colour; we make it, one at a time, only when it’s wanted. Less waste. More heart. Something real you couldn’t find anywhere else. It didn’t exist until you imagined it.

Aim high. We’ll meet you there.

Our name

Where the name comes from: a song that promised “I can reach any star,” and a character who spent a lifetime becoming better than before.

Reach Any Stars began with a song. “Faith of the Heart,” the hopeful opening theme of Star Trek: Enterprise, has a line that stuck: “I can reach any star.” It’s about believing you can get somewhere better than where you started.

The spirit comes from a character, too: Andrew Martin, the one Robin Williams plays in Bicentennial Man, who spends a lifetime striving to become more than he was built to be. Better than before. That idea is the whole point of what we make.

Our mark is a delta holding a star. In maths and science, a delta (Δ) means change: the distance between where you are and where you could be. The star is what you’re reaching for. Put together, they say it in one shape. Change, aimed at something worth reaching.
